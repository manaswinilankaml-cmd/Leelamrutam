In the peaceful village of Vrindavan, little Krishna was famous for one thing — stealing butter.

The gopis of the village would carefully hide their butter pots high on shelves, thinking Krishna could never reach them. But Krishna was clever.

One day, he gathered all his friends and whispered, “Today we shall rescue the butter!”

They formed a human pyramid. One boy stood on another’s shoulders, and Krishna climbed to the very top. Laughing mischievously, he reached the pot and grabbed a handful of fresh white butter.

Just then, the gopi who owned the house walked in.

“Krishna!” she shouted.

But Krishna only smiled, his face covered in butter. He shared the butter with his friends and even fed some to the monkeys outside.

When Mother Yashoda heard the complaints, she tried to scold Krishna.

But when she looked into his innocent eyes, her anger melted away.

For everyone in Vrindavan knew the truth — even Krishna’s mischief was filled with divine joy and love.
